# AP Calculus BC — prep & game map

**AP Calculus BC** is the College Board’s **single-variable + series** calculus course (with AB as a subset). The exam blends **multiple-choice** and **free-response** (FRQs); **series**, **parametric/polar/vector**, and **differential equations** are **BC-specific** emphases beyond AB.

This page is an **unofficial** prep **overview** and a **map to levels in First Principles**. It does not replace a textbook or CB materials.

---

## Big idea buckets

| Bucket | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| **Limits & continuity** | \(\varepsilon\)–\(\delta\) light; asymptotes; continuity at a point |
| **Differentiation** | Rules; implicit; inverse trig; related rates mood |
| **Applications of derivatives** | Extrema, MVT, curve sketching, optimization |
| **Integration** | Antiderivatives, u-sub, parts, partial fractions mood; improper integrals |

| **Parametric, polar, vector-valued** | \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) from parametric; polar area & arc length; motion |
| **Sequences & series** | Convergence tests; Taylor/Maclaurin; error bounds |

## Polar coordinates (BC exam staple)

- **Cartesian link:** \(x = r\cos\theta\), \(y = r\sin\theta\).
- **Area:** \(\displaystyle A = \frac12 \int_\alpha^\beta r^2\,d\theta\).
- **Arc length:** \(\displaystyle s = \int_\alpha^\beta \sqrt{r^2 + \left(\frac{dr}{d\theta}\right)^2}\,d\theta\).

In-game polar stages plot **\(r(\theta)\)** with \(\theta\) along the horizontal axis as a teaching simplification before you convert to \((x,y)\).

---

## Circle & implicit work

Standard **circle:** \((x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = R^2\). **Implicit differentiation:**

\[
\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{x-h}{y-k} \quad (\text{where } y\neq k \text{ on the full curve}).
\]

The game’s **circle** level uses the **upper semicircle** \(y = k + \sqrt{R^2-(x-h)^2}\) so the path remains a single \(y(x)\) graph for one traversal.

## Game levels that echo BC motifs

Look for titles prefixed **BC:**, **Polar:**, and **Circle** in level select, e.g.:

- **Inverse / transcendental:** arctan, \(\ln x\), \(\sqrt{x}\), \(\tan x\) window, \(b^x\), \(e^{kx}\), Maclaurin \(\cos x\), logistic DE, cubic inflection, \(\sinh x\).
- **Polar:** cardioid, rose.
- **Geometry:** circle (upper arc).
- **Plus** earlier **Taylor / Maclaurin** (\(e^x\), \(\sin x\)), **geometric series**, **multivariable slices**, **Riemann** rules—all useful BC intuition.
